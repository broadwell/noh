Plot
The ageuta is the last module of the Shite re-enters part, and although we heard him in the preceding shōdan, his entrance is completed in this module as the drape around the tsukurimono is removed.
Text
Set in hiranori, the text, based on a poem from the Shinsen Rōei-shū by the T’ang poet Fang Kan, is sung by the jiutai.
Music
The beginning of the shōdan is clearly articulated with a change of color in the vocal part as the jiutai replaces the shite in singing the chant.
Visuals

The focus is on Square 8 where the drape around the tsukurimono is about to be removed.


This ageuta has two points of intermedia interest: a missing nohkan’s pattern, and the first dance-to-text movement (kata).

As it is typical of an ageuta, its first sentence is repeated, its iteration being preceded by an uchikiri during which one would expect to hear the nohkan’s takane pattern. It is the absence of the pattern that brings the listeners to attention, as the drape around the tsukurimono is about to be removed.

In a context where so little is happening as it is the case when a shite is sitting, small details gain in importance. For instance, as the jiutai sings “beyond the hedge, the autumn mountains”, the shite slides to the right to better admire them . Although this mimicking dance movement is the first one in the play, it will abound in the coming kuse.

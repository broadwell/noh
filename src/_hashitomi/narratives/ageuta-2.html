Form
Second Act - Shite Re-enters:
Issei → Sageuta → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
While poetry is sung the Yūgao spirit reveals itself.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.

The text is based on a poem from the Shinsen Rōei-shū by the T’ang poet Fang Kan.

Music
The chant by the jiutai set in hiranori is accompanied by percussion instruments in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The focus is on the dwelling located on Square 8 where half way through the shōdan the drape is slowly removed and the shite is revealed. The shite remains partly obstructed by the lattice gate of the tsukurimono.

The ageuta is the last module of the Shite Re-enters dan and its highest intermedia point. Although we heard the shite sing in the preceding shōdan, his slow but dramatic appearance is completed as the drape around the tsukurimono is removed. The spirit has now a visual identity.

As it is typical of an ageuta, its first sentence is repeated, its second iteration being separated by an uchikiri. This process of reiteration creates momentum for the subsequent melodic motion from high to the higher note called kuri. The first words of the fourth line ‘Shu u sho o’ allude to Autumn developing the theme of sadness that appeared in previous modules.

This conicindes with the first mimetic movements of the second act. In a context of minimal motion such as when a shite is sitting, small details gain in importance. For instance, as the jiutai sings “beyond the hedge, the autumn mountains”, the shite slides to the right as if to better admire them .

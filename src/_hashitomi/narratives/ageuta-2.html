Synopsis
While poetry is sung by the jiutai the curtain is lowered and removed, and the spirit of Lady Yūgao reveals itself.
Text
The text is based on a poem from the Shinsen Rōei-shū by the T’ang poet Fang Kan.
Music
Sung by the jiutai, the vocal part is in congruent rhythm set in hiranori. The beginning of the shōdan is marked by a striking change of vocal color from shite solo to the jiutai.
Visuals

The focus is on the dwelling located on Square 8 where half way through the shōdan the drape is slowly removed and the shite is revealed. He remains partly obstructed by the lattice gate of the tsukurimono.


The ageuta is the last module of the Shite re-enters part, and although we heard him sing in the preceding shōdan, his appearance is completed in this module as the drape around the tsukurimono is removed.

This ageuta has two points of intermedia interest: a missing nohkan’s pattern, and the first dance-to-text movement (kata).

As it is typical of an ageuta, its first sentence is repeated, its iteration being preceded by an uchikiri during which one would expect to hear the nohkan’s takane pattern. It is the absence of the pattern that brings the listeners to attention, as the drape around the tsukurimono is about to be removed.

In a context where so little is happening as it is the case when a shite is sitting, small details gain in importance. For instance, as the jiutai sings “beyond the hedge, the autumn mountains”, the shite slides to the right to better admire them . Although this mimicking dance movement is the first one in the play, it will abound in the coming kuse.

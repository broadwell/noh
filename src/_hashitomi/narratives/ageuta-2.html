Form
Second Act - Shite Re-enters:
Issei → Sageuta → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
While poetry is sung by the jiutai the curtain is lowered and removed, and the spirit of Lady Yūgao reveals itself.
Text
Regular rhythmic poetry.

The text is based on a poem from the Shinsen Rōei-shū by the T’ang poet Fang Kan.

Music
The chant by the jiutai set in hiranori is accompanied by percussion in strict rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The focus is on the dwelling located on Square 8 where half way through the shōdan the drape is slowly removed and the shite is revealed. He remains partly obstructed by the lattice gate of the tsukurimono.

The ageuta is the last module of the Shite re-enters part and its highest intermedia point. The jiutai picks up the congruent singing accompanied by the strict percussion. Although we heard the shite sing in the preceding shōdan, his slow but dramatic appearance is completed as the drape around the tsukurimono is removed and he begins to do some mimetic movements.

The beginning of the shōdan is marked by a striking change of vocal color from shite solo to the jiutai.

As it is typical of an ageuta, its first sentence is repeated, its iteration being preceded by an uchikiri during which one would expect to hear the nohkan’s takane pattern. It is the absence of the pattern that brings the listeners to attention, as the drape around the tsukurimono is about to be removed.

In a context of minimal motion like when a shite is sitting, small details gain in importance. For instance, as the jiutai sings “beyond the hedge, the autumn mountains”, the shite slides to the right as if to better admire them . This first mimetic movement in the second act will lead to many more in the coming kuse.

The shite slowly traverses the bridge, as he moves toward the Mirror room, where in preparation for the second act, he will change kimono. Even, as he walks out off the stage, the shite stays in character. The slow and quiet exit is well suited for a spirit of a lady.


The shite’s exit is sometimes accompanied with music. This is the case for instance, when the shite is an important character such as a deity like in Kokaji. Hashitomi’s shite is a young lady, hence her slow and dignified exit from the stage is unaccompanied, yet quite poignant as if time had been suspended. Her exit lasts a minute and a half.

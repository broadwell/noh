Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakaru-2 → Ageuta-1
Synopsis
The monk continues to ask the apparition about her identity. She reveals to be no longer alive but confirms being always found at a certain mansion.
Text
Prose and arhythmic poetry.
Music
An exchange between the waki and shite alternates between kotoba and sashinori chant, accompanied by the kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi’s parts in a flexible rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The use of stage-space remains static with the shite standing at Square 1, and the waki kneeling at Square 5. The shite alternates between facing the waki and the audience.

The percussion instruments play in flexible setting, offering a slow, sparse and open accompaniment appropriate for an unmetered duo. The monk singing in a non-congruent fashion, a sashinori chant to be more precise, insists that the shite reveals her name. The shite resists, replying in kotoba as if to stress her position. Yet, while keeping her actual identity secret, she goes as far as to confirm that she is no longer alive . The kakaru concludes with the following two lines:

“At a certain mansion,
I could always be found, indeed it is so.”

Those familiar with the Genji monogatari will remember that Lady Yūgao’s dwelling is described as surrounded with a fence filled with ‘evening face’ flowers. Although at this point in the play the actual identity of the shite’s character’s identity is still unknown, one could speculate about it, as the monk appears to be doing. Indeed, the last two lines form a single sentence that should have been sung by the woman. Yet, it is the monk who sings the first line, suggesting that he may have a hunch about her identity. The sentence is then completed by the woman in singing mode, as she turns towards the monk, as if acknowledging his intuition. This coincides with a change in the character of the percussion material, moving from sparse mitsuji to the regular tsuzuke pattern, a switch that subtly increase the density emphasizing the shōdan’s closing and raising expectation about the one to come.

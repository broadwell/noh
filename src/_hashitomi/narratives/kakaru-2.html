Form
First Act - Dialogue:
Kakeai → Kakaru-2
Synopsis
The monk continues to ask the apparition about her identity. She reveals to be no longer alive but confirms being always found at a certain mansion.
Text
Prose and arhythmic poetry.

It makes a reference to what will be understood as Lady Yūgao's mansion from Genji Monogatari : Chapter 4: 'The Twilight Beauty.'

Music
An exchange between the waki and shite alternates between kotoba and sashinori chant, accompanied by the kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi’s parts in a flexible rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The use of stage-space remains static with the shite standing at Square 1, and the waki kneeling at Square 5. The shite alternates between facing the waki and the audience.

The last shōdan of the 'Dialogue' section in Act 1 stays rather simple but sees the intermedia texture enriched by the return of the drums accompaniment, and by the mixing of kotoba and chant. The dramatic intensity grows as a new clue for the identity of the main character is introduced.

The percussion instruments play in flexible setting, offering a slow, sparse and open accompaniment appropriate for a free conversation. The monk, insists on the shite to reveal her identityby singing in a non-congruent fashion. Replying in kotoba, the shite resists but goes as far as to confirm that she is no longer alive . She keeps her actual identity secret.

The conclusion of the kakaru consists of two lines:

“At a certain mansion,
I could always be found, indeed it is so.”

They refer to Lady Yūgao’s mansion whose fence was covered with ‘evening face’ flowers, and as such, give a clue to the shite's identity. The fact that it is the monk who sings the first line suggests that he may be guessing who the apparition is. The sentence is completed by the woman in a singing mode, as she turns towards the monk. This coincides with a change in the character of the percussion material , moving from sparse mitsuji to the regular tsuzuke pattern. This switch subtely adds momentum to the music as important information is being presented.

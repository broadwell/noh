Plot
The priest keeps on pressing the shite to reveal his identity.
Text
The non-congruent text with a syllabic structure of 7 + 5, includes a reference to the Lady Yūgao's mansion from Genji Monogatari : Chapter 4: The Twilight Beauty.
Music
Alternatively sung and spoken dialogue between the priest and Lady Yūgao. The two percussion instruments provide a flexible obbligato.
Visuals

The use of stage-space remains static throughout the shōdan with Lady Yūgao standing at Square 1, and the priest kneeling at Square 5.


The scene is shaped by the combination of story-telling and the way the text is used to bound together the two protagonists.

The first act’s ‘Dialogue’ part closes with a kakaru, whose beginning is clearly marked by the entrance of the percussion instruments in flexible setting. The priest singing in a non-congruent fashion, presses the shite to reveal her identity. Replying in kotoba, the shite goes as far as to confirm that she is no longer alive , but keeps the mystery on her actual identity. The conclusion of the kakaru deserves a special mention. The last sentence is “At a certain mansion, I could always be found, indeed it is so.”. This is a reference to Lady Yūgao’s mansion whose fence was covered with ‘evening face’ flowers, and such, a clue on the shite’s identity. Thus, this line should have been delivered by the shite, yet it is the priest who sings its first part, completed by the shite in singing mode, . It is interesting that as the mystery on the shite’s identity was about to be partially lifted, the text assignment ends up creating some ambiguity.

This important clue on the shite’s identity coincides with a switch in the percussion material from sparse mitsuji to the regular tsuzuke pattern , a move that adds momentum to the music and call the listener to attention, as important information is being presented.

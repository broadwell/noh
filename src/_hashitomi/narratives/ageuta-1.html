Plot
The ageuta is the last module of the Shite re-enters part, and although we heard him in the preceding shōdan from this part, his entrance is fully completed in this module as the drape around the tsukurimono is removed.
Text
Set in hiranori, the text, based on a poem from the Shinsen Rōei-shū by the T’ang poet Fang Kan, is sung by the jiutai.
Music
The beginning of the shōdan is clearly articulated with a change of color in the vocal part as the jiutai replaces the shite in singing the chant.
Visuals

The attention is focused on Square 8 where the drape around the tsukurimono is about to be removed.


This ageuta has two points of intermedia interest: a missing nohkan’s pattern, and the first dance-to-text kata, that will abound in the coming kuse.

First, the module ( shōdan ) brings in a new and refreshing sound since it is sung by the jiutai, the last performer who had not yet taken part in the action. It plays the role of a narrator and provides another hint on Lady Yūgao’s identity when it refers to ( gojō atari ), that is the ‘neighborhood of the fifth street’, where according to the Genji Monogatari ), Lady Yūgao’s dwelling was temporarily located. Moreover, anticipating the exit of the shite, we hear that the shite ‘Evening face’ (Lady Yūgao) is disappearing in the flower’s shadow, referencing the way she first appeared to Genji, as alluded to in the second kakaru.

It is also the first shōdan involving singing with the two percussion instruments and the nohkan, producing thus far the play’s richest texture. Being set in hiranori, it is also the first module in the play with congruent singing, accompanied by the percussion instruments in strict setting. Hence this is the first shōdan involving a relationship between syllabic setting of the text and the eight-beat measure (honji).

Finally, this shōdan is the first one to involve stage movements that are related to the text. It is common for an ageuta’s first sentence to be repeated, its iteration being usually preceded by an uchikiri during which the nohkan player performs the takane pattern. While the jiutai signs the module’s third sentence stating that “In the moment the eyes turn aside, she becomes a dream.”, Lady Yūgao takes six steps forward, in preparation for a Right Circlet that she performs to mimic her disappearance, that the jiutai is about to annouce: “…as she disappears in the shadow of the flowers.”. As the melodic line, supported by the nohkan’s mi roku no ge pattern, moves from the high range to the medium one, preparing to cadence in the low range, the shite performs the standard sequence of movements Forward pointing and Open-retreat, and exits the stage.

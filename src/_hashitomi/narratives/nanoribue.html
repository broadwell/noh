Form
First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanoribue → Nanori → Kakaru-1
Synopsis
Arrival of a monk.
Text
n/a
Music
Nohkan solo.
Visuals
The waki dressed in humble monk clothes enters the stage and stops at Square 1. He is followed by the kyōgen in a local townsman costume who sits down at the back of the bridge opposite Pine 1. The kyōgen would usually enter inconspicuously in the middle of the first act during the first ageuta. In Hashitomi, the first ageuta concludes the act and is followed immediately by the shite’s exit. Thus, the kyōgen enters earlier, inconspicuously together with the waki, not to interfere with the shite's exit.

The play opens with the entrance of the monk accompanied by a nohkan solo. The nohkan player performs it in a plain fashion that colors the monk with modesty, and suggest a play with a lighter tone. The monk's unpatterned costume in a single color gives a feeling of subdued and subtle elegance.

The simple instrumentation of this unmetered melody, the absence of text, and the casual and predictable movements provide this shōdan with a low Intermedia Index, a common way of starting the first shōdan of a play’s first dan.

It is the nohkan player’s responsibility to loosely adapt the pacing to the waki’s walking speed so that the performance of the entrance music coincides with the waki’s concluding kata : the Step back.

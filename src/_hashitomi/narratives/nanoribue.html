Form
First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanoribue → Nanori → Kakaru-1
Synopsis
Arrival of a monk.
Text
n/a
Music
Nohkan solo.
Visuals
The waki dressed in humble monk clothes slowly enters the stage and stops at Square 1. He is followed by the ai-kyōgen in a local townsman costume who sits down at the back of the bridge opposite Pine 1.

The entrance of the monk is the opening of the play. It starts a typical Waki Enters section. As such, and fitting well with tradition of very gradual build-up in Noh, it is very calm and simple. Before the first arch of raising energy and intermedia richness develops, the movement is slow and predictable, the music is limited to one instrument playing languidly without a beat, the entering actor is a secondary one and there is no text or chant.

The simple instrumentation of a nohkan solo is also appropriate for the entrance of a modest monk. In the next shōdan he will not reveal his name but only the name of the temple that he is from. The nohkan plays the nanoribue in a plain fashion to underline the relative lightness of the play and modesty of the monk. The monk's unpatterned costume in a single color gives the feeling of subdued and subtle elegance.

It is the nohkan player’s responsibility to loosely adapt the pacing to the waki’s walking speed so that the performance of the entrance music coincides with the waki’s concluding kata: the Step back.

[In preparation for the ai-kyōgen, the kyōgen would usually enter inconspicuously in the middle of the first act during the first ageuta. In Hashitomi, the first ageuta concludes the act and is followed immediately by the shite’s exit. Thus, the kyōgen enters earlier, inconspicuously together with the waki, not to interfere with the shite's exit.]

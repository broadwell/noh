Form
Second Act - Shite Performs:
Kuse → Jonomai
Synopsis
Poetic account of scenes from the romantic encounter of Lady Yūgao and Prince Genji. It includes the lovers overhearing Buddhist chanting at night as well as being introduced by a iugao flower delivered on a white fan.
Text
Poetry in irregular rhythm.

The poetic text includes two references to Genji Monogatari, both from letters written by Lady Yūgao.

Music
The chant set in hiranori is sung by the jiutai with one line (ageha) sung by the shite. The kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi’s parts in strict rhythmic setting are joined by a non-congruent nohkan part.
Visuals
The kuse is the first module in the play that extensively uses dance-to-text movement. The gradual expanded use of the stage-space follows the overall three-part subdivision of the shōdan. In Part-I the performance of most kata happens on Square 9. The use of stage-space is extended to Squares 2 and 3 in Part-II, and finally to the stage’s right lateral – Squares 1, 2, and 3, and center Squares 4, 8, and 9 during Part-III.

The Shite Performs section consisting of kuse and the following jonomai dance, together with the final kiri are the high points of intermedia richness and dramatic energy of the whole play. The three shōdan are also typically the longest within the play. Most of the performers are active, music is to a large extent congruent, the movement adds significant mimetic dimension and most of the stage is used.

The kuse has three particular points of intermedia interest. The first one is concerned with how mimicking kata are used to provide the text with visual representation. The second looks at the fluid identity of shite and jiutai as the text and movement keep redefining their roles. The third point considers how various musical techniques are used to emphasize phrases in the text. This includes the use of range, rhythmic patterns, and irregular measures.


Intermedia Part-I

The flower ritual that the monk is performing brings back memories to Lady Yūgao about the prayer to Buddha that she and Genji heard the very first night they spent together. Part I is divided into two sub-parts: The first one provides context as the jiutai is narrating the story, whereas in the second sub-part refers to Lady Yūgao’s emotions.

The text of the first sub-part introduces Genji and his short romance with Lady Yūgao. As it is common for a kuse, the melody starts in the low range when a Left Stamp on the seventh beat of the third honji triggers a short and sudden incursion in the high range over the words “Tada karibushi no yo mo sugara/Through the night they heard from next door a pilgrim’s voice in prayer”, thereby emphasizing the importance of the heard prayer. Then, a Right Stamp on the seventh beat of the following honji triggers a return to the medium and subsequently low range.

At this point, the text refers to the “pilgrim’s voice in prayer”, hence the shite makes a Right Step pivot and takes Three Steps Forward and one Step Backward as to better position herself to listen to the prayer. She then performs a ‘Mind listening’ kata by bending her upper body forward.

As the jiutai is about to recite the sutra “Namutōraidōshi Mirokub’to zo tonaekeru”, the kotsuzumi player plays his hajiki pattern which include an off-beat attack that immediately follows the ōtsuzumi’s stroke on the first beat. This rarely used pattern with its oddly positioned attack creates a syncopated bouncing effect that calls for the listener's attention, preparing the mood for the coming prayer. The sutra includes 21 syllables, set in a sequence of four measures: toriji (4-beat measure), kataji (6-beat measure), toriji (4-beat measure), and a honji (8-beat measure), for a total of 22 beats or 44 syllable-slots. As an outcome of this larger than needed number of beats, the delivery speed of the text slows down resulting in an appropriate pacing for the recitation of a prayer.


Intermedia Part-II

As it is standard in dances, the Opening Fan kata marks the beginning of the dance’s next section, in this case Part-II. The expanding use of the space is also indicative of the beginning of a new section. Indeed, while the performance of kata in Part-I was located around Square 9, it expands to Squares 2 and 3 in Part II.

In the first sentence of Part II, Lady Yūgao tells the monk about her first encounter with Genji, hence it positioned her in the present. Lady Yūgao reminisces about three specific moments, each one shares the same melodic shape and the same transformation in its mode of delivery. More specifically, each of the three melodic lines start in the medium range and cadence in the low, and each one starts in the declamatory mitsuji-utai accompanied by sparse mitsuji patterns before switching to tsuzuke-utai accompanied by steady tsuzuke patterns.

Lady Yūgao’s first memory refers to the moment Genji first saw her house. The second one refers to Genji beckoning his foster brother Koremitsu to pick up the ‘evening face’ flower for him. By performing the Beckoning Fan kata at the same instant as she recalls the moment, the shite becomes Genji. Then, the jiutai speaking Genji’s words orders his foster brother to pick up the white flower. By extending the fan and turning to face the tsukurimono, the shite is still Genji who identifies the flower his foster brother is to pluck.

Finally, the last and third memory refers to the white fan that foreshadows Genji and Lady Yūgao’s encounter. According to the Genji Monogatari, a young servant girl stepped out of the house and provided a guard rather than Koremitsu with a scented white fan and told him, as he was about to pluck the flowers, “Here give them to him on this – their stems are so hopeless”. Although no narrated, this moment of the story is summarized with the shite turning to face front and performing the Offering the Fan kata, hence for that short gesture, the shite is the young servant. Then, the jiutai tells that the flower was picked and presented. Hence, the shite becomes Koremitsu as he performs a sequence of mimicking kata: Left Hand pick up of the flower, Laying of the flower on the fan, Kneeling and the Two Hands Offering of the Fan.


Intermedia Part-III

At the beginning of Part III the shite becomes Genji in a moving moment. This is the sole time in the kuse when shite sings (ageha). In Kneeling Position he is looking at his fan and chants: “Genji, gazing at it intently”.

Jiutai then speaks for Lady Yūgao, when it tells that it is while contemplating a flower at her gate that Genji noticed her in the distance, and that their love bloomed out of a flower presented on a fan. From that moment, the jiutai starts speaking for Genji and alludes to the poem he wrote on the fan, but the narration is interrupted by the elegant dance: jonomai. Hence, one has to wait until the next shōdan , the waka to hear Genji’s love poem.

The jiutai’s reply coincides with the entrance of the nohkan’s pattern takane mi kusari. It starts with a five-syllable line stretched over the honji’s eight beats, thereby emphasizing its text “Of you passing in the distance…”. The shite who performs at the same time a Large Zigzag, as if passing in the distance, becomes Lady Yūgao. The kata takes her from Square 2 to the dynamic Square 4 where she concludes with a Scooping Point followed by an Open-retreat, thereby opening Part III by further expanding the use of the stage-space.

The melodies corresponding to the text “Of you passing in the distance…” and “… and if I had not answered about the flower” are both set in the declamatory mitsuji-utai, which has the effect of increasing momentum and adding a dramatic stress to the words. The following line of twelve syllables delivered in tsuzuke-utai is set over a honji preceded by a toriji (four-beat measure), hence creating a slowdown in the declamation that emphasizes the dramatic words: “forever we must have remained strangers.”.

The dance is also gaining in momentum. The kata Take the Corner brings the main character to a strong position on Square 3. This is where over the words: "by the fan touched by our hands" the shite mimics the text with a Fan to the Left hand kata. After a Large Left Circle (Squares 3 – 9 - 1), the sequence ends on Square 1.

An interesting identity switch takes place at this point as the jiutai starts singing Genji's words. Thus, once on Square 1, and over the lines “…in a joyous pledge of love…”, the shite performs a Feather Fan kata, as he takes Nine Steps towards the waki. By offering him the fan, he becomes Genji, while the waki for this brief moment stands for Lady Yūgao.

Finally, the shite initiates the closing sequence that will take him center stage after having traversed the front stage lateral. The sequence starts with a Renesting the Fan, followed with a dynamic Take the Corner with Extended Fan, and a Large Circle (3-5-9) that takes him to Square 9, in front of the tsukurimono, where he performs a Small Zigzag followed by a Closure scoop , a standard sequence of kata used for closing a section.

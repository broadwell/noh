Plot
The yet unidentified spirit of Lady Yūgao appears as a smiling flower, while the monk is performing the service for flowers.
Text
n/a
Music
Entrance music with flute in non-congruent and percussion in flexible rhythmic settings. The is the first module featuring all musicians. The music is very slow, poignant and evocative. Nohkan's reserved ornamentation sets a humble yet refined tone.
Visuals

The waki moves from the center-stage (Square 9) to his resting post at (Square 5), at which point the shite starts a slow entrance concluded at the First Pine. The red elegantly designed costume suggests that the character is a young woman from a high-status family. The patterns of flowers and fences on the front will gain their full meaning as the narrative reveals it.


The entrance of the shite is preceded by very slow but intense music that builds up anticipation of the mysterious appearance. Only the percussion accompanies the waki as he walks to his pole. The expression of his gait serves as a preparation, it helps establish the right energy to bring in the shite. The nohkan begins to play at the second honji. Its haunting melodic patterns ‘summon’ the apparition and help establish its mysterious beauty.

The timing and gesture of curtain lifting depends on the shite. After waiting for the right moment, the way the shite utters omaku suggests to the stage assistant the manner, in which the lifting is to be executed. In the featured performance the shite waited until the third honji, which gave more time for the music to set the proper mood. As he enters the bridge, he purposely avoids facing the audience delaying the moment he will show the mask's full face. He walks very slowly, taking three minutes to reach the First Pine. At this point, the curtain goes down as slowly as it was lifted. Only as he is about to sing , does he turn toward the audience, thus revealing the mask that can be admired as the entrance music is coming to an end.

For the entire shōdan, the free-flowing nohkan’s melody is supported by an equally free percussion obbligato consisting only of sparse mitsuji patterns played in flexible mode. The absence of periodic pulsations provides the music with an unsettling feeling, which matches well the spirit of a dream and an otherworldly appearance.

Visuals
The tsukurimono, a rectangular cube covered with a curtain and with a thatched roof on top. It is brought in by two stage assistants and placed in front of the two percussionists on Square 8. It will be later revealed that inside it is hidden the shite and that it represents a delapidated dwelling. Its symbolic entrance gate is hung with a hashitomi, the source of the play's title. Hashitomi means ‘half shitomi’, an oblong wooden panel shutter, the upper edge of which is hinged and the loose lower end can be pushed open. Iugao evening flowers and gourds creep over it.

Subtle mystery is created by the two stage assistants who very slowly and carefully bring in an unusual tsukurimono, a cube representing a dwelling that may be covering something with its curtain.

The tsukurimono is brought in by two stage assistants.


Two stage assistants bring in a tsukurimono representing the entrance gate to a house. It is hung with hashitomi, and evening flowers and gourds creeping over it. Hashitomi means ‘half shitomi’, an oblong wooden panel shutter, the upper edge of which is hinged and the loose lower end can be pushed open. It is placed in front of the two percussionists on Square 8.

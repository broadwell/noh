The tsukurimono, a rectangular cube covered with a curtain and with a thatched roof on top representing a delapidated dwelling is brought in by two stage assistants and placed in front of the two percussionists on Square 8.


Two stage assistants bring in very slowly a rather mysterious object, tsukurimono a rectangular cube covered with a curtain and with a thatched roof on top representing a delapidated dwelling. It will be later revealed that it inside it was hidden the shite and that it contained a symbolic entrance gate to a house. The gate is hung with hashitomi, and evening flowers and gourds creeping over it. Hashitomi means ‘half shitomi’, an oblong wooden panel shutter, the upper edge of which is hinged and the loose lower end can be pushed open.

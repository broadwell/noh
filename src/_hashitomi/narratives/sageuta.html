Form
Second Act - Shite Re-enters:
Issei → Sageuta → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
Continuation of poetry heard from the inside of the dwelling, now invoking a metaphor of tears by refering to snow-dampened sleeves.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.

The poetic text is based on a poem by Po Chū-i (772-846).

Music
The chant of the shite is set in hiranori is accompanied by the kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi in strict rhythmic setting, joined by a non-congruent nohkan part.
Visuals
The attention of the audience reamins to be drawn to the symbolicdwelling from within which the shite continues to sing.

The module is a further step of increased intermedia energy and a transition of rhythmic organization, register, and mood from the issei to the following shōdan, the ageuta.

The rhythmic setting of the chant becomes congruent. Moreover, the starting low register of the sageuta chant completes the issei chant’s descending register trajectory from high to medium, while its own motion up towards the medium range serves as a transition towards the high range of the next shōdan, the ageuta. In comparison to the issei’s change from shite to jiutai, the sageuta is sung by the shite, but the following ageuta, the last shōdan of the Shite re-enters part, will be delivered again by the jiutai.

As the text of the second act is gradually moving from factual information to poetry, a nostalgic tone sets in. The process started with the issei’s last line: “Rain dampens Genken's (Yüan Hsien) gate.”, where rain is a metaphor for tears. It is carried over in the sageuta which start with another metaphor for tears: dampened sleeves. This will then be carried over in the next shōdan, the ageuta with a reference to the limpid moon soaring over a desolate evening. In fact, one has to wait until the rongi to understand the source behind this sadness when the shite asks “When will they meet again?”, a clear and poignant reference to the lovers yearning for each other.

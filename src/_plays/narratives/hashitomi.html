Plot

First act: A monk performing a ritual for flowers notices a ...Second Act: Memories of the first encounter of Lady Yûgao with Prince Genji. He met her on his way to visit a mistress Lady Rokujô. The prince noticed an unfamiliar white flower on the fence of the house he was passing. As he stopped to admire it, he noticed a beautiful lady in the house. He sent her a white flower resting on a fan. It came back with an inviting poem written on it, to which Genji replied with a poetic invitation of his own that led to a passionate yet short affair. The Japanese name of the flower is yûgao translated in English as 'evening face'.

Libretto of Kokaji

Act-1

Waki Enters: Nanori
Wakizure:
Waki-tsure:Somo kore wa
Ichijō-no-In ni tsukae-tatematsuru.
Tachibana-no-Michinari to wa waga koto nari.
Sate mo mikado kon’ya kidoku no go-reimu, mashimashite.
Sanjō-no-Kokaji Munechika ni.
Mi-tsurugi wo utaserarubeshi to no
senji tadaima narikudarite sōrō hodo ni.
Kono yoshi wo Munechika ni mōshi-tsukebaya to zonji sōrō.
Wakizure:
To begin with,
I am in the service of the Emperor Ichijo.
I am known as Tachibana no Michinari.
It so happens that his Lordship
has had a portentous dream oracle.
The Sanjo swordsmith Munechika
must be ordered to forge a blade.
I am on my way now to tell Munechika of this matter.
Waki Enters: Mondō 1
Wakizure:
Ikani kono uchini Munechika no watari-sōrō ka
Wakizure:
Is Munechika at home?
Waki:
Tare nite watari sōrozo.
Waki:
Who is it that has come here?
Wakizure:
Kore wa senji nito sōrō
Waga kimi Kun’ya fushimi no reimu, mashimashite.
Mitsurugi wo utaserarubeshi tono onkoto nari.
Tōtō tsukamatsuri sōrae
Wakizure:
I am a messenger from the Emperor.
His Majesty has had a marvelous oracle in a dream.
According to the oracle you must forge a fine sword for him.
Please comply with this order at once.
Waki:
Senji kashikomatte uketamawari sōrō sarinagara.
Orifushi aizuchi utsubeki mono naku sōrō wo ba,
nani to tsukamatsuri sōrō beki.
Waki:
While I certainly must agree to execute this imperial order,
as I do not have anyone to assist me,
I do not know how I will be able to carry it out.
Wakizure:
Fushigi no koto wo mōsu mono kana.
Sono na wo etaru, nanji naruga
aizuchi utsubeki mono no naki towa.
Kokoroegataki iigotokana.
Wakizure:
What you say, sir, seems most strange.
To hear that someone of your reputation
does not have an appropriate assistant,
is most unfortunate news.
Waki:
Kore wa ōse nite sōrae domo,
Kayō no ichidaiji no mono wo tsukamatsuru ni wa,
ware ni hodonaru koto no mono no aizuchi, tsukamatsuri koso.
Mitsurugi wo mo uchimōsubekere.
Tonikaku ni onpenji wo mōshikane.
Sekimenshitaru bakari nari.
Waki:
Even though I am commanded to do so,
without an assistant whose skill is equal to my own,
it will not be possible fulfill this order
to forge a fine imperial blade.
This is the answer I must give,
though I am overcome with embarrassment.
Wakizure:
Mōsu tokoro wa saru koto naredomo.
Mikado mo fushigi no reimu mashimaseba.
Ikanaru koto ka sōrōbeki
tanomoshiku omoi mōshitsutsu
hayahaya ryōjō mōlsubeshito.
Kasanete senji arikereba
Wakizure:
Even though it may be as you say,
yet as this is the Emperor’s wonderful dream oracle,
it surely must be relied upon
and you must quickly say
you will accept the order.
Let me repeat the imperial command!
Waki Enters: Ageuta 1
Waki:
Kono ue wa tonimo kakunimo Munechika ga,
Waki:
At this point, in any case, Munechika,
Jiutai:
Tonimo kakunimo Munechika ga,
Shintai koko ni kiwamarite.
Mitsurugi no yaiba no midaruru kokoro narikeri.
Sarinagara goseitō sugunaru ima no miyo nareba,
moshimo kidoku no ariyasen.
Sore nomi tanomu kokoro kana.
Sore nomi tanomu kokoro kana.
Jiutai:
In any case, Munechika,
must here decide his course of action.
Like the ripples beaten along a well-forged blade his heart wavers.
Yet as the reign in the present age is righteous,
a miracle may well be afforded me.
I must place my trust only in this.
I must place my trust only in this.
Shite Enters: Dialogue
Wakizure:
Senji ni makase tōtō mitsurugi wo tsukamatsuri sōrae.
Wakizure:
Hurry! make the sword.
Waki:
Kashikomatte sōro.
Waki:
Yes! sir.
Shite Enters: Monologue
Waki:
Kore wa ichidaiji no koto wo, ōse idasarate sōrō mono kano.
Kayō no koto ni wa shinriki wo tanomu naradewa, bechigi naku sōro.
Soregashi ga uji no shin na Inari no, myōjin nite sōrō hodoni.
Kore yori sugu ni, Inari ni mairi.
Kono koto kisei mōsabaya to zonji-sōro.
Waki:
This is a great task you have come to give me.
Such a command can be carried out if I ask for divine help.
As my family deity is Inari no Myojin,
I will go at once to Inari,
and pray concerning this matter.
Dialogue: Mondō 2
Shite:
Nō nō are naru wa Sanjō no Kokaji Munechika nite, on iri sōrō ka.
Shite:
Ho there! Is that the Swordsmith of Sanjo, Munechika ?
Waki:
Fushigi ya na nabete narazaru on-koto naru ga.
Michi mo naki kata yori kitaritamai
waga na wo sashite notamō wa.
Ikanaru hito nite mashi-masu zo.
Waki:
How mysterious! Here is someone who is certainly not an ordinary person.
There is no path from the direction from which he has appeared,
and now he has called me by name.
What manner of man are you ?
Shite:
Kumo no ue naru kimi yori mo
tsurugi wo uchite mairaseyo to
nanji ni ōse ari shi yo nō.
Shite:
You have been ordered
by his Imperial Majesty
to forge a sword!
Waki:
Sareba koso,
sore ni tsukete mo nao nao kidoku no on-koto nare.
Tsurugi no choku mo tadaima naru wo.
Hayaku mo shiroshimesaruru koto
Kaesu-gaesu mo fushin nari.
Waki:
As I thought, you are not what you seem to be,
and events must be of great importance indeed.
I have just received the order to make a blade!
How could it be that anyone could know so soon ?
No matter how I consider it, the matter is most strange.
Shite:
Geni geni sore wa saru koto naredomo
ware nomi shireba moro bito made mo.
Ten ni koe ari, chi ni hibiku.
Shite:
In truth, it is as you say. It is said: To know a thing one's self,
it is to find it is known by all.
A voice in heaven, echoes on the earth!
Dialogue: Ageuta 2
Jiutai:
Kabe ni mimi:
Iwa no mono iu yo no naka ni,
Iwa no mono iu yo no naka ni,
Kakure wa araji koto ni nao,
kumo no uebito no mitsurugi no
hikari wa nanika kurakaran.
Tada tonome kono kimi no
Meguni ni yoraba mitsurugi mo
nado ka kokoro ni kanawazaru.
Nado ka wa kanawazarubeki.
Jiutai:
Wall have ears:
In the world where rocks may speak,
In the world where rocks may speak,
and nothing is hidden,
nothing shall dim the radiance
of the Emperor's sword.
Only have faith
that through his Majesty's grace and the sword
will be realized as you wish.
It must be realized.
Shite Performs: Kuri
Jiutai:
Sore Kan-nō sanseki no tsurugi
inagara Shin no midare wo shizume.
Mata Yō-tei ga Kei no tsurugi
Shiujt'n no hikari wo ubaeri.
Jiutai:
Once with such a blade, the Emperor's Three-Foot Sword,
subdued the rebellion of the Ts'in by its reputation alone.
Again, the Yang Emperor, with his sword Kei
outshone the brilliance of the House of Chou.

joined his spirit after death with the virtue of his sword,

Even evil spirits and demons
were in fear of that sword's glow,

Dialogue: Rongi
Jiutai:
Mata waga chō no sono hajime.
Ninnō jiunidai Keikō-tennō ,
miko no mikato no on-na wo ba
Yamata-dake to mōshishiga.
Kiui wo Taiji no chiku wo uke.
Seki no higashi mo harukanaru.
Azuma no tabi no michi sugara.
Ise ya Owari no umizura ni tatsu nami made mo
kaeru koto yo to urayami,
itsu ka warera mo kaeru nami no,
koromode ni arama shi to omoi-tsuzukete-yuku hodo ni.
Jiutai:
And so, it was in the early days of our history,
the Twelfth of the Imperial Line, the Emperor Keiko,
had a son known by the name Yamato-dake.
The wild barbarians! he received the order to subdue them.
The barrier check points far behind as yet further to the East.
The road of his journey to the Eastern provinces took him.
Even at Ise and Owari the waves on the sea rise and return!
With envy he wondered always,
when he too might find himslef, with foam-drenched sleeves
returning home, even as he continued on his way.
Shite:
Koko ya kashiko no tatakai ni,
Shite:
In the battles in one place and in the next ,
Jiutai:
Jin-ba gankutsu ni mi wo kudaki,
Chi wa takuroku no kawa to natte,
kōha tate nagashi sudo ni oyoberu Ebisu mo.
Kabuto wo nuide hoko wo fuse
mina kōsan wo mōshikeri.
Mi-koto no gyō yori mi-kariba wo susume tamaeri.
Koro wa Kami-na-zuki,
hatsu ka amari no koto nareba.
Yo-mo no momiji mo fuyugare no
tō-yama ni miyuru hatsu-yuki wo,
nagame sase tamaishi ni,
Jiutai:
Men and horses: their bodies crushed in their caverns,
their blood on the battlefields a river,
their shields swept away on crimson waves again and again.
Finally, the barbarians removed their helmets,
lay down their arms and were made to surrender.
From the time of this Prince's reign, the royal sport of hunting was begun.
It was an incident that occurred,
on the twentieth day of the Tenth month:
Everywhere the autumn tints had faded,
and even on the distant winter withered mountains,
the traces of the first snow fall could be seen.
Shite:
Yebisu shi-hō wo kakomitsutsu.
Shite:
The barbarians surrounding him on every side.
Jiutai:
Kare-no no kusa ni hi wo kake
yo-en shikiri ni moe-kitari.
Kataki-seme-tsuzumi wo uchi-kakete.
Ka-en wo hanachite kakarikareba.
Jiutai:
Set fire to the grass of the withered fields, and as the blaze burned high
and as the blaze burned high with heavy lingering billows of smoke.
Began to beat their war drums in the signal to attack.
The Towering flames! as the attack blazed forward.
Shite:
Mi-koto tsurugi wo nuite...
Shite:
The Prince drew his sword....
Jiutai:
Mi-koto tsurugi wo nuite,
atari wo harai tachimachi.
ni honō mo tachi-shirizoke to,
shi-hō no kusa wo nagi-haraeba.
Tsurugi no seirei arashi to natte,
hon wo mo kusa mo fuki-kaesarete.
Ten ni kakayaki chi ni michite.
Myō-ka wa kaette kataki wo yakeba.
Su man gi no Yebisu-domo wa,
tachimachi koko nite usetengeri.
Sono nochi shi-kai osamarite,
Jin-ka to-zashi wo wasureshi mo.
Sono Kusanagi no yue to ka ya.
Tadaima nanji ga utsubeki
kono zuisō no mitsurugi mo.
Ika de sore ni wa otorubeki.
Tsutōru ie no Munechika yo,
kokoro yasuku mo omoite ge-kō shi tamae.
Jiutai:
The Prince drawing his sword,
wielded it about him.
At once, the flames drew back,
as it mowed down the grasses in all directions.
The spirit of the sword became a storm
blasting back the raging flames and grasses too.
The blaze lit up the heavens, covered the earth!
A furnace of fierce flames turned back to devour the enemy forces.
Thus, the countless barbarians,
and cavalrymen were brought down.
So, in later years the Empire was at peace:
So much so that the people forgot to bar the doors of their houses.
This is because of the 'Grass-cutter', it was said.
And now, this auspicious omened blade
that you must forge!
Is there any reason it should be a lesser blade than that ?
Munechika, heir to a house of proud tradition,
set your heart at rest and go home now.
Shite Exits: Kakeai 1
Waki:
Kanka honchō ni oite tsurugi no itoku
Toki ni totte no shiugen mōsu, hakari no kusōrō.
Sate satemo onmi wa ikanaru hito zo.
Waki:
What you have told me of the power of the swords in China and Japan
has gladdened my heart at this important hour.
Yet, I press you again to tell me who you are ?
Shite:
Yoshi tare nari tomo tanomubeshi.
Mazumazu choku no mitsurugi wo
utsu beki dan no kazaritsutsu.
Sono toki ware wo machitamawaba.
Shite:
Who I may be is of no consequence, but you must pray!
Before anything else adorn your forge mound
where you will temper the Imperial blade.
And at that time, if you will but wait for me.

Machitamae to.
Iugumo no Inariyama,
yukue mo shirazu usenikeri.
Yukue mo shirazu usenikeri.

I will change into supernatural form.
Having changed into a supernatural form,
I will come without fail, at the appointed time,
and join you, adding my strength to yours.
Then wait for me!
So saying, into the evening clouds of Mt. Inari,
without a sign of where he went, he vanished.

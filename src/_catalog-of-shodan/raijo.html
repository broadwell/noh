Catalog of Shōdan: Entrance and Exit Music

Raijo

Percussion
Flexible and Strict
Nohkan
Non-congruent and Congruent

Raijo is an instrumental piece. The featured instrument is the nohkan, accompanied by the three percussion instruments whose respective rhythmic patterns merge into a single macro-pattern. In this performance, it is after a full honji that the percussion patterns get settled.

Raijo is composed of two sections: The first one called nakairi-raijo, is slow and non-congruent, creating a stately mood for the exit of one or two characters. Its macro-rhythm shows that its beginning is strongly articulated with a taiko dainobachi stroke on the first beat followed immediately with a ta stroke on the kotsuzumi.

{% include image.html src="/assets/images/shodan/Figure-1-NOBckgr.png" %}

The tempo picks up at the beginning of the second section which is congruent. This one called kyōgen-raijo accompanies the entrance of the ai-kyōgen.

The kyōgen-raijo tempo is faster and being congruent, the kakegoe are shorter. Its lighter tone differs from the kyōgen-raijo’s solemn feel, and its macro-rhythm with its equidistant eight beats is less complex and steadier, its second half being an almost repetition of the first, if it was not for the kotsuzumi’s chi stroke on the 6th beat.

{% include image.html src="/assets/images/shodan/Figure-2-NOBckgr.png" %}

It is against the percussion’s static macro-rhythm that the nohkan performs its melody. The non-congruent first part, which accompanies the exiting shite and waki, covers five honji, with each segment starting around the second beat, using the even beats as reference time point for his sub-segments, in contrast with the percussion strokes on odd beats.

The congruent second part, that support the ai-kyōgen’s entrance, it is spread over four honji and a half.

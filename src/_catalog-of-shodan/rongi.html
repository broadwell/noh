Rongi

Voice
Congruent
Percussion
Strict
Nohkan
Non-congruent

Set in hiranori the rongi is a sung dialogue between two actors, or between an actor, usually the shite, and the jiutai. Although positioned in the second act, Hashitomi’s rongi compares well with the one that commonly closes the first act when the shite alludes to or reveals his identity.

When the rongi closes the first act no nohkan pattern is played. In the case of Hashitomi, the rongi leads to the kuse, thus the nohkan player links the two by performing the mi roku no ge pattern.

Kuri

Non-congruent

A high-pitched melodious and short einori chant sung by the shite, or the jiutai, or alternatively by both. Kokaji’s kuri is set for jiutai accompanied by ōtsuzumi, kotsuzumi and nohkan.

Kuri's text is based on a sequence of 7 + 5 syllables and yet, it classified as an arhythmic text. This comes from the fact, that one must be able to hear the subdivision of a text into two hemistiches for it to be considered a rhythmic text. The overall complex rhythmic organization of the kuri makes it impossible to hear the text as being organized into two groups.

Kuri is classified among the Introduction chants, so an explanation about its 'introduction' nature must be addressed since it is rather ambiguous. Kuri's narrative serves as a lead into the following shōdan, the sashi, whose narrative is then fully developed in the next kuse. Thus, it is because it leads to the kuse that it is classified as an 'Introduction' chant.

Often the kuri follows a shidai-chant or issei-chant when sung by an actor, and precedes a sashi when sung by the jiutai, as it is the case in Kokaji. It always closes with its signature melisma called hon-yuri.

The nohkan performs two patterns during the kuri. The first one, also called kuri is short. It is followed by the longer pattern called yuri.

Kokaji's kuri is sung in tsuyogin style. For the most part, its melody is sung on the Medium/High tone. Yet, the module's name refers to the high pitch kuri; thus, the melody of Kokaji's kuri includes four melodic motions towards the kuri tone. They appear as part of embellishments, and are highlighted in bold in the video's text. Finally, the signature closing melisma hon-yuri closes the chant on the Low tone.

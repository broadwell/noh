Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
Exit of the shite and waki followed by the entrance of the kyōgen actor.
Text
None.
Music
Full hayashi perform exit music with percussion in flexibile and nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings. It is seemlessly followed by entrance music with percussion in strict rhythmic setting and the nohkan continuing in non-congruent setting.
Visuals
The shite takes over two minutes to exit. When he reaches the Second Pine the waki stands up and folows him. After they both have left, the ai-kyōgen enters. The curtain is raised for each of them individually.

In clear contrast with the preceding ageuta, the raijo is non-congruent during the exit of the two protagonists, but congruent during the ai-kyōgen’s entrance. The shite is the first one to exit. He is followed by the waki who does not enter the bridge until the shite has exited. Moreover, the ai-kyōgen does not enter the stage until the waki’s exit has been concluded by a closed curtain.

The featured instrument in the raijo is the nohkan, accompanied by the three percussion instruments whose respective rhythmic patterns merge into a single macro-rhythmic pattern. Since it is not possible to predict how long the exit and entrance will last, musicians follow the mihakarai principle. This is a concept of flexibility that allows the number of repetitions to be determined by the action on stage. In this case, it is determined by the time the actors need to exit and enter the stage. The taiko player who sits in front of the bridge, introduces a sequence of shōnobachi strokes, and shortens his kakegoe to communicate to the other musicians the move to congruency.

At the beginning of the raijo, the shite takes three special footsteps, each one performed solemnly, separated from the next one by eight beats. Each step symbolizes the shite traveling a long distance at a very fast speed. After the third step, the shite walks out in standard fashion.

Form
First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
Exit of the shite and waki followed by the entrance of the kyōgen actor.
Text
None.
Music
Full hayashi perform exit music with percussion in flexibile and nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings. It is seemlessly followed by entrance music with percussion now in strict rhythmic setting and the nohkan continuing in non-congruent setting.
Visuals
The shite takes over two minutes to exit. When he reaches the Second Pine the waki stands up and folows him. After they both have left, the ai-kyōgen enters. The curtain is raised for each of them individually.

The Ageuta 3 conluded by the nohkan's shrilling hishigi pattern set up an exciting tone for the deity’s vanishing. The raijo’s releases the energy.

Its slow and non-congruent first part accompanies the exit of the two protagonists, while its congruent second part supports the kyōgen’s entrance. The shite is the first one to leave. He is followed by the waki who does not enter the bridge until the shite has left. After both of the protagonists exit and the curtain is closed behind them, the curtain is lifted again to let enter the the kyōgen actor.

The nohkan is the featured instrument. It is accompanied by the three percussion instruments whose respective rhythmic patterns merge into a single macro-rhythm. Since it is not possible to predict how long the exit and entrance will last, musicians follow the mihakarai principle. This is a concept of flexibility that allows the number of repetitions to be determined by the action on stage. In this case, it is determined by the time the actors take to exit and enter the stage. The taiko player who sits in front of the bridge, introduces on the seventh beat a sequence of three sonobachi strokes, and shortens his kakegoe to communicate to the other musicians the move to congruency and the increase in tempo.

At the beginning of the raijo, the shite takes three special footsteps, each one performed solemnly, synchronized with the kakegoe ‘ho’ on beats 2.5 and 6.5. Each step symbolizes the shite traveling a long distance at a very fast speed. After the third step, the shite walks out in standard fashion.

First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanori → Mondō-1 → Ageuta-1 → Dialogue
Synopsis
Under pressure from the Imperial envoy, Munechika accepts to forge the blade.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the waki and wakizure delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The wakizure is kneeling at Square 5 and the waki at Square 9.

The energy of the dialogue is constrained by the kneeling position and minimal movement of the two actors, and lack of musical accompaniment.

Considering that this is the first out of three consecutive unaccompanied shōdan, the instrumentalists put aside their instruments and members of the jiutai, their fans. Because the waki’s role in Kokaji is substantial, it is the wakizure who is from now, and for the rest of the play sitting at the waki’s pole (Square 5).

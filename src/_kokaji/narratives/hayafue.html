Form
Second Act - Shite Re-enters:
Hayafue
Synopsis
The appearance of a deity at Munechika's forge.
Text
None.
Music
The three percussion instruments in strict and nohkan in congruent rhtyhmic setting.
Visuals
The waki walks off the dais to Square 6 to await the entrance of the shite. After some time the shite enters wearing an ko-tobide mask with bulging eyes and a bushy wig crowned with a figure of a fox that identify him as a diety. In his right hand he is holding a hammer. He walks rapidly to the First pine, where he performs standard Open Retreat kata.

This extremely dynamic entrance music announces the arrival of a powerful being, a deity, in Kokaji’s case. The hayafue features a congruent nohkan part accompanied by the three percussion instruments in strict rhythmic setting. This is the first shōdan in the second act with the full hayashi. The Intermedia Index has risen again.

The melody begins with a one-honji- introduction that starts with the nohkan’s shrilling hishigi pattern. Paired with the fast tempo, it creates a heroic tone that leads to the Hayafue’s melody which lasts seven honji. It is played two times and a half, before moving to its two-honji cadencing tome

It is at the very end of its second iteration that the curtain is lifted with a fast and energetic motion and shite makes his dramatic entrance onto the bridge. His left arm is stretched forward holding a hammer. He delays entering the stage proper however and first performs the standard sequence of kata: Forward Pointing and Open-retreat at the First pine. It is there that he stands at the conclusion of the hayafue, still holding the left arm stretched building anticipation that will be resolved at the beginning of the next shōdan, when he finally enters the stage proper.

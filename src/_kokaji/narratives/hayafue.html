Synopsis
An extremely dynamic entrance music announces the arrival of a powerful being, a deity in the Kokaji’s case.
Text
None.
Music
The kotsuzumi, ōtsuzumi, and taiko’s parts in strict rhythmic setting accompany the first congruent nohkan part, consideringg that the raijo was partially congruent.
Visuals
It is almost at the end of the hayafue that the shite enters the bridge holding in his right hand a hammer that he is pointing forward. In Male style, he reaches the First pine, where he stands, momentarily delaying his entrance on the stage proper.

The hayafue is a congruent nohkan solo accompanied by the three percussion instruments. Its entrance completes the hayashigoto in preparation for the upcoming dance.

The melody begins with a one-honji long introduction that starts with the shrillingg hishige tone (notated in the score as'Hi'), which paired with the fast tempo, provides the melody with a heroic tone. It lasts seven honji, and it is played two times and a half, before moving to its two-honji cadencing tome pattern.

It is at the very end of its second iteration that the shite makes his entrance on the bridge but delays entering the stage proper by performing a Forward pointing followed by an Open-retreat at the First pine where he stands at the conclusion of the hayafue, thereby creating a visual tension that will be resolved at the beginning of the next shōdan when he will finally enter the stage proper.

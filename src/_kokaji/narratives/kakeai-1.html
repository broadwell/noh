First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
Munechika acknowledges the encouragement that he has drawn from hearing about the legendary Chinese and Japanese swords. He asks the young boy to reveal his identity but is told instead to pray, prepare for the forging and wait.
Text
Prose.
Music
Exchange between the shite and the waki delivered in kotoba accompanied by percussion in flexible rhythmic setting. Final few words are sung in non-congruent setting in tsuyogin style.
Visuals
The shite and waki are back to their initial kneeling positions on Square 9 and 6, respectively. The shite turns toward waki for the last two lines of the text.

The shōdan marks a sharp return to one of the simplest textures: recitation of prose accompanied only by percussion using sparse mitsuji patterns.

At the conclusion as the boy stands on his knee and dramatically announces that he will return he switches to chanting in tsuyogin style, thus producing a serious and commanding tone. The highlighting is reinforced also by a Raising on knee motion.

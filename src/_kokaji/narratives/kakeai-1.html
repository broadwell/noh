Plot
Munechika indicates that hearing of the legendary Chinese and Japanese swords has gladdened his heart. Yet he presses the young boy to reveal his identity. The latter evades the question by suggesting that Munechika should prepare his forge’s mount and waits for his return.
Text
Earthly text set in kotoba.
Music
Kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi in flexible setting.
Visuals
The boy and Munechika are back to their initial kneeling position: Square 9 for the former, Square 6 for the latter.

The shōdan marks a sharp return to one of the simplest textures: reciting voices with factual text accompanied by sparse mitsuji patterns. It also presents an interesting point of intermedia interest related to mode of delivery.

At the conclusion of a spoken exchange during which Munechika confirms that he accepts the imperial request, the young boy switches to non-congruent singing in tsuyogin mode, thus producing a serious and commanding tone, as he stands on his knee and dramatically announces that he will return . The change of mode of delivery combined with a raising motion highlight this important text, which links this shite with the one who will be coming in the second act.

First Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-1 → Ageuta-3 → Raijo
Synopsis
Munechika acknowledges the encouragement that he has drawn from hearing about the legendary Chinese and Japanese swords. He asks the young boy to reveal his identity but is told instead to pray, prepare the forging and wait.
Text
Prose.
Music
Text delivered in kotoba accompanied by the kotsuzumi and ōtsuzumi in flexible rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The boy and Munechika are back to their initial kneeling position: Square 9 for the former, Square 6 for the latter.

The shōdan marks a sharp return to one of the simplest textures: reciting voices with factual text accompanied by sparse mitsuji patterns. It also presents an interesting point of intermedia interest related to mode of delivery.

At the conclusion of a spoken exchange during which Munechika confirms that he accepts the imperial request, the young boy switches to non-congruent singing in tsuyogin mode, thus producing a serious and commanding tone, as he stands on his knee and dramatically announces that he will return . The change of mode of delivery combined with a Raising on knee motion highlight the importance of the text. Moreover, it also links this shite with the one who will be coming in the second act, whose overall look will be quite different.

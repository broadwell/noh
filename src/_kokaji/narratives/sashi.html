Form
First Act - Shite Performs:
Kuri → Sashi → Kuse
Synopsis
The young boy continues to tell a story of a late minister's soul giving power to the emperor Huan Tsung's blade.
Text
Arhythmic poetry.
Music
The shite’s chant is succeeded by the jiutai’s, both sung in tsuyogin style of a text set in sashinori. They are accompanied by percussion in strict and the nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic settings.
Visuals
The shite and the waki are kneeling on Squares 9 and 6, respectively. For the final line of text they turn toward each other after which, the shite turns toward the audience.

CAN WE PUT THIS PARAGRAPH IN THE CATALOG OF SHODAN? The sashi’s overall texture is quite complex since the chant and nohkan parts are non-congruent, accompanied by the two-hand percussion set in flexible rhythm. In this context, it is the percussionists’ responsibility to adapt their sparse mitsuji patterns to the chant whose tempo is in constant flux.

WHERE DOES THIS GO? IT IS NOT INTERMEDIA. The text focuses exclusively on Emperor Huan Tsung (713 – 742 AD) who dreamed about his late Minister Chung K’ei coming to his defense with a sword.

Although the young boy and jiutai alternate in singing, this is not a true dialogue since the jiutai sings the boy’s words lecturing Munechika.

The Intermedia Index rises slightly with the shite chanting in addition to the jiutai. Their exchange is purely formal as the jiutai speaks the shite's mind. An aditional boost of energy is provided in the conclusive words about the swords: “and their miraculous power is beyond expression!” by the shite rutning to face the waki and by the nohkan introducing the kuse no ryō pattern.

Plot
TThe noriji-2 functions like an introduction to the dance. The hayashikata is now complete and the shite has entered the Bridge. During this shōdan, he will enter the stage proper ready to start the play’s pure dance, the maibataraki.
Text
Sequences of text in average of eight syllables per honji set in ōnori. sung by jiutai in tsuyogin mode. It speaks for the shite who is pressing Munechika to get started with the forging of the blade.
Music
The kotsuzumi, ōtsuzumi, and taiko’s parts are set strict setting paired with a non-congruent nohkan’s part.
Visuals
Standing at the first pine, the energetic deity opens the shōdan with a series of tension-building kata before getting to Square 1, the starting position for the pure dance.

The main feature of this shōdan is the shite’s grandiose entrance on the main stage supported by the full hayashikata and the jiutai, who speaks his words.

The noriji-2 begins with an introduction that further delays the shite’s entrance on the main stage. Still standing at the First pine, the deity signals that he is about to make his move with a sequence of Six stamps, that he concludes with a Left Turn (now facing the stage) Forward Pointing, and its usual companion kata, the Open-retreat, leading to the nohkan’s Takane hane pattern, which concludes the introduction.

The deity finally enters the stage with a Large Zigzag performed in the dynamic Martial mode. At the conclusion of this kata, the deity faces Munechika as the jiutai intones "utsubeki jisetsu wa kōku ni shireri" (The appointed time for forging is known), pressing the waki to get started on his mission.

From this point on, the shite performs a sequence of kata that brings him to Square 1, where he concludes the noriji-2 with the standard sequence: Forward Pointing and Open-retreat.

Plot
The swordsmith Munechika is at the Inari Shrine praying for a divine intervention to assist him in meeting an imperial order, when a mysterious boy appears out of nowhere.
Text
Dialogue between the shite and waki with factual text set in kotoba.
Music
Unaccompanied dialogue.
Visuals
Starts across the entire stage-space, the waki kneeling at Square 6, the shite out of sight, still in the mirror room.

This shōdan has two points of intermedia interest related to the use of space and text setting.

The shite entrance intended as a surprise, is not introduced by any entrance music. Moreover, the shite who is meant to come as an apparition to the waki is not even visible to the audience, delivering his first line from the mirror room, a call from the distance called yobikake. The vowels of his first few lines are extended to give an impression that this uncanny voice is coming from the distance. The prolongation process of the vowels gradually tempers off as the shite reaches the stage.

The dramatic tension created by the unexpected and otherworldly shite’s appearance is heightened by the fact that it opens up a dialogue between two actors separated by the widest possible distance , the shite making his way from the mirror room to the stage while the waki is standing on Square 6.

As the shite who personifies a young boy, finally reaches Square 1 and turns to face the Munechika, there is a sudden change of tone and style that smoothens the transition to the next shōdan, whose poetic text is sung. Thus, the two performers switch to non-congruent singing to deliver the shōdan’s last two lines.

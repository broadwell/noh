Form
First Act - Dialogue:
Mondō-2 → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
Munechika prays at the Inari Shrine for divine assistance in forging the blade. A mysterious boy appears out of nowhere and surprises Munechika with the knowledge of the Imperial order.
Text
Prose.
Music
Unaccompanied exchange between the shite and waki delivered in kotoba.
Visuals
The waki is standing on Square 6 facing the mirror room with the lifted curtain from where the voice of the shite is heard. The shite then enters and walks over the bridge to Square 1.

The intermedia interest of the scene comes from the use of stage space and text setting.

With waki standing on Square 6 facing the shite, who at first is out of sight in the mirror room, the widest possible distance between them creates a unique dramatic effect. Hearing but not seeing the shite helps the listeners share in Munechika's surprise, as he notices in consternation that "... there is no path from the directon from which this unusual person has appeared." To allow for the surprise 'appearance', the shite's is not introduced or accompanied by entrance music. Shite's first line is a call from the distance called yobikake. The vowels are extended to give an impression that the mysterious voice is coming from afar. The prolongation of the vowels gradually tapers off as the shite reaches the stage.

As the young boy finally reaches Square 1 and turns to face Munechika, there is a sudden change of tone and style during the last two lines of the shōdan. The actors switch to non-congruent singing to share a sentence; waki starting with "A voice in heaven" is conluded by shite's "echoes on the earth." The switch smoothens the transition to the next shōdan, whose poetic text is also sung.

Form
First Act - Dialogue:
Mondō-2 → Ageuta-2
Synopsis
Munechika is told to have faith and forge the requested sword.
Text
Poetry in regular rhythm.
Music
Chant of the jiutai in yowagin style, set in hiranori with accompaniment of hayashi. The percussion is in strict and the nohkan in non-congruent rhythmic setting.
Visuals
This shite performs some basic kata and a modest opening of the stage space around Squares 1.

THIS PARAGRAPH WILL NEED TO END UP IN LEVEL 1: Following a sequence of unaccompanied shōdan delivered in spoken mode, the first ageuta with its accompanied poetic text came across as the closing highlight of the Waki enters section. The situation is the same with the second ageuta, which itself follows three shōdan delivered in spoken mode. Its accompanied poetic text paired with the first dance movements make it the closing highlight of the Shite enters section.

The shōdan concludes the Dialogue dan. It raises the Intermedia Index through the introduction of congruent singing accompanied by percussion. Also, the dramatic expression is hightened by the use of stage movement and kata to mark structural points aligned with musical changes, and mimetically reinforce the psychology of the narrative.

The whole shōdan is chanted by the jiutai who serves as a narrator and gives voice to the boy. Munechika gradually gets accustomed to the boy's unexpected appearance as they turn toward each other at three structural points. First, they face each other at the beginning of the shōdan when Munechika notices the boy, then, at the conclusion of the first part, when the boy walks toward Munechika and hails the radiance of the Emperor's sword, and finally, at the end of the shōdan, when we hear that the forging order must be followed. The second part is marked also by the nohkan who joins with the naka no takane pattern, reinforcing the enouragement given to Munechika with words: tada tonome kono kimi no/"only have faith."

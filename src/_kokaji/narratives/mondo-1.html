Plot
The waki-tsure, an Imperial envoy arrives at the waki’s house, the swordsmith Munechika, where delivers a sovereign order.
Text
Dialogue between the waki and waki-sure with factual text set in kotoba.
Music
Unaccompanied dialogue.
Visuals
The action takes place on the bridge that represents Munechika’s house. The waki stands at the third pine, the waki-tsure at the first one.

The imperial envoy calls for Munechika who appears at the door step of his house (at the curtain), and bows to the Emperor’s envoy. The dialogue that follows is delivered in kotoba.

Upon hearing that he is to forge a sword for the Emperor, Munechika is trying to find a way to decline the request. Getting exacerbated the envoy delivers his last line: “Let me repeat the imperial command”, with a forceful style of singing called suteru, stressing his order with a commanding step towards the waki.

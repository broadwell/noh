First Act - Waki Enters:
Nanori → Mondō-1 → Ageuta-1
Synopsis
The Imperial envoy arrives at swordsmith Munechika’s house, where he delivers the sovereign's order to forge him a blade.
Text
Dialogue with factual text set in... sanbun.
Music
Unaccompanied dialogue between the waki and wakizure in kotoba
Visuals
The action takes place on the stage-bridge that represents the entrance to Munechika’s house. The waki stands at the third pine, the wakizure at the first one.

The imperial envoy calls for Munechika who appears at the door step of his house and bows to the Emperor’s envoy. The dialogue that follows is delivered in kotoba.

Upon hearing that he is to forge a sword for the Emperor, Munechika is trying to find a way to decline the request. Getting exacerbated the envoy delivers his last line: “Let me repeat the imperial command”, with a forceful style of singing called suteru, stressing his order with a commanding step towards the waki.

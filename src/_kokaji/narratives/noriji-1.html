Form
Second Act - Waki Waits:
Notto → Noriji-1
Synopsis
The waki kneeling on the dais is waving his sacred wand as the jiutai speaks for him. While motion on stage is minimal, the music moves to the next level of congruency.
Text
Sequences of text in average of eight syllables per honji set in ōnori sung by jiutai in tsuyogin mode.
Music
The kotsuzumi, ōtsuzumi, and taiko’s parts are set strict setting.The entrance of the taiko represents the first step towards a complete hayashigoto.
Visuals
The waki is kneeling on the dais at Square 4.

The intermedia point of interest of the shōdan is at the formal level, as we look at how the noriji correlates with its two adjacent shōdan. The analysis shows that while the visual component appears rather static, the music gains in momentum.

The noriji-1 is visually static, setting the proper tone for the delivery of a prayer. Munechika remaining on the dais starts waving his sacred wand as the jiutai, speaking for him, calls for divine help. On the other hand, the music is building momentum with its faster tempo and larger instrumentation. First, the chant is now sung by the jiutai compared to a single voice in the preceding notto. Moreover, the taiko has now joined in. Although it participated to the performance of the raijo, it is for the first time in the play that it participates to the accompaniment of a chant.

Set in congruent ōnori, the text counts in average eight syllables per honji, in net contrast with the notto’s non-congruent chant.

Finally, it is interesting to consider the fact that this noriji precedes the hayafue, the entrance music for the second-act shite who personifies a divine being. Even in Noh, the advent of a deity is a miracle, and a miracle can only be created when the power of prayers gets to a climactic level. Thus, the building up of the momentum in the music leads towards the dramatic line ‘kinjo-saihai’ (the word for prayer) sung first by Munechika, concluded by the jiutai, setting the tone for the dynamic hayafue and the entrance of the second-act shite.

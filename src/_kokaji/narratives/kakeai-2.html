Form
Second Act - Shite Exits:
Kakeai-2 → Kiri
Synopsis
Munechika and the deity complete the forging of the blade by inscribing their names; Munechika the Swordsmith on one side, and Little Fox on the other.
Text
Prose.
Music
An exchange between shite and waki delivered in kotoba accompanied by ōtsuzumi and kotsuzumi in flexible rhythmic setting.
Visuals
The two protagonists remain on the dais located on Square 4 and mimick stamping their names on the blade with the hammers.

The Intermedia Index drops significantly. Musically the kakeai-2 is like a moment of respite before a storm. For the span of three honji, there is no nohkan, taiko or jiutai. The chant is spoken by solo voices of shite and waki. The strict rhythmic synchronization of singing and instruments that was found in the preceding three noriji and will return in the next and last shōdan, the kiri, is temporarily interrupted. The percussion instruments are set in flexible rhythm following five and before the final shōdan, all in strict rhythm

The kakeai-2 is very much a continuation of the noriji-3 in terms of coordination between text and kata. We hear and see the two performers completing the forging by engraving their respective signatures on the blade .
